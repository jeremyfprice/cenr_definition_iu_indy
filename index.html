Overview

Community-engaged research has emerged as a vital approach to scholarship that challenges traditional academic boundaries by fostering genuine partnerships between universities and communities and holds a critical position in Pillar 2 of the Indiana University system-wide and Indiana University Indianapolis 2030 Strategic Plan. However, the field has long struggled with inconsistent definitions and unclear evaluation criteria that have hindered its recognition and advancement within academic institutions.

The Community Engaged Research Faculty Circle (CERF-C) at Indiana University Indianapolis developed a comprehensive framework for community-engaged research (CEnR) through collaborative definition-building and analytical mapping of the fieldâ€™s conceptual landscape.

The Community Engaged Research Faculty Circle

The Community Engaged Research Faculty Circle (CERF-C) is a faculty working group established in 2020 to advance the practice and scholarship of community-engaged research at Indiana University Indianapolis. CERF-C comprises faculty members from diverse disciplines who share a commitment to community engagement and collaborative research.

Core Definition

The definition was developed through a two-stage process. In the first stage, members of the Community Engaged Research Faculty Circle drafted and refined candidate language through multiple rounds of discussion. In the second stage, the draft definition was subjected to a Delphi validation process with community stakeholders. This sequence ensured that the statement reflects both disciplinary consensus among faculty and validation from community partners, providing a definition that is conceptually robust and contextually grounded.

Selected terms are accompanied by badges indicating their relative scores across three analytic dimensionsâ€”Foundation, Integration, and Leverage. These annotations reflect the conceptual analysis underpinning this framework, signaling how certain elements function as core, connective, or strategically influential within community engaged research at IU Indianapolis.

Community engaged research is scholarship carried out through reciprocal partnerships between researchers and community members grounded in shared power and ownership. It is oriented toward collaborative inquiry and community priorities, leverages community wisdom, produces meaningful change, and generates a variety of outcomes and outputs that advance well-being and knowledge.

Seven Guiding Commitments

1. Relational Investment

CEnR requires more time and sustained effort than traditional approaches to research to build trusting, collaborative relationships, which are essential to fulfilling the university's mission.
2. Reciprocal Benefit

Community engaged research creates conditions for mutual growth and transformation among all partners: researchers, community members, and organizations. Rather than simple exchanges, these partnerships foster shared learning, collective capacity building, and deepened relationships that strengthen all participants and their ability to create positive change together, both during and beyond the research process.
3. Values-Driven Practice

CEnR efforts are accountable to core values and commitments, including belonging, mutuality, transparency, and shared power and ownership while remaining anchored to the requirements and assumptions of the research methodologies employed.
4. Holistic Integration of Scholarship and Pedagogy

Although CEnR is foremost a research activity, many engaged scholars integrate their research and teaching. Student growth and developmentâ€”through authentic experiences and classroom practiceâ€”are woven into the work.
5. Product Pluralism

CEnR results in a wide range of productsâ€”from the conventional scholarly article, conference presentation, or book chapter to the community-facing action plan, policy document, curriculum, public outreach effort, exhibit, or other intermediate and summative productsâ€”that are all considered valid and value-adding.
6. Knowledge and Material Outcomes

CEnR aims both to co-create new knowledge and share it in ways that provide tangible value to partner communities, by offering actionable insights, fostering new perspectives, or contributing to meaningful benefits in their lives and their circumstances.
7. Partnership Pathways

Community engaged research encompasses a spectrum of partnership approaches, from including communities in research processes to supporting fully community-owned research efforts. This trajectory recognizes that meaningful engagement can occur at multiple levels, allowing researchers and communities to find approaches that align with their capacity, interests, and goals while maintaining core commitments to reciprocity and shared benefit.
